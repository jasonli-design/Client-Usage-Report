Optimization Analysis — TNT_Eurosport & Bleacher Report

TNT_Eurosport

Daily API calls (M) — note weekend spike pattern

Optimization Signature — Late March Baseline Drop TNT_Eurosport shows a strong weekly cycle: weekend spikes (Sat/Sun) driven by live sports, with weekday troughs. After mid-March peaks (Mar 14-15 reached an all-time high of 45M on a Sunday), the last week of March (Mar 23–30) shows a dramatically compressed floor. Weekday baselines drop to 1.99M–5.72M — roughly 40–60% below typical weekday averages seen in Jan/Feb. This compression of the weekday baseline while weekend patterns persist is consistent with an optimization that reduced redundant polling or caching API calls on non-event days.

Bleacher Report

Daily API calls (M) — look for post-peak stabilization

Optimization Signature — Feb 12 Step-Change Bleacher Report grew rapidly from Jan (3.1M avg) through early Feb, reaching a peak of 21M on Feb 5. Then, beginning Feb 12, there is a clear and sustained step-change downward — the weekly average drops ~32% from 13.72M to 9.91M and stabilizes in the 10–12M band through late February. This is the hallmark of a server-side or client-side optimization: growth continues but at a new efficiency level. Volume then rises again in March as real usage grows, but without the "noise" that drove the Jan–early Feb runup.
